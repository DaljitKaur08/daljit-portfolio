Rutherford Gold Foil Experiment

The Rutherford experiment changed our understanding of the atom. Alpha particles were aimed at a thin gold foil. Most passed through, but some were sharply deflected — revealing that atoms contain a tiny, dense nucleus.

  • Most particles passed straight → atoms are mostly empty space.
  • Some deflected → positive charge repelled alpha particles.
  • A few bounced back → direct hit with the nucleus.
